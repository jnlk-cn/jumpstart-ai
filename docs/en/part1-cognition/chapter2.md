# Chapter 2: Why "Getting In First" is a Rational Strategy

## What You'll Learn

After reading the previous chapter, you know AI application development isn't as unattainable as you thought. But I guess you're still hesitant—"I know the barrier isn't as high as I thought, but I still don't feel ready."

This is normal. I've seen too many people delay for half a year or even a year on "preparation," and by the time they feel ready, the opportunity window has long closed.

This chapter will thoroughly explain: **in AI application development, "getting in first" is not gambling—it's the most efficient rational strategy.**

Specifically, you will understand:

- Why AI technology skill half-life is ridiculously short, and by the time you're "ready," the opportunity has passed
- Why companies really want "people who can do the work" rather than "people who know everything"
- Why learning while working is 2-3x more efficient than studying behind closed doors
- Why the feeling of "I'm not qualified enough" is an illusion, and how to live in harmony with it

---

## 2.1 AI Application Development Skills Have Extremely Short Half-Lives

Let me share something I witnessed firsthand.

In October 2022, when LangChain was just released, a friend of mine spent a full 3 months mastering this framework, built a dozen demos, and wrote clearly on his resume: "LangChain Expert." At the beginning of 2023, he successfully job-hopped with a 40% salary increase using this.

Then what happened? In 2024, the LangChain team released LangGraph with a completely different architecture. In October 2025, LangChain directly released version 1.0, rewriting the entire framework from scratch. What do you think happened? Most of what he learned in those 3 months became obsolete.

**This is the harsh reality of AI application development: what you're learning may become outdated before you've even finished learning it.**
