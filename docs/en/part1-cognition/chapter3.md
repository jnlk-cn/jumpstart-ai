# Chapter 3: Your Entry Roadmap

## What You'll Learn

In the first two chapters, we solved the problems of "can I enter" and "should I enter now." This chapter is the conclusion of the Cognition section, and I'll give you a **roadmap you can follow directly**.

After reading this chapter, you will know:

- What type of entry candidate you are (backend/full-stack/beginner/student), and your unique strategy
- 8-week intensive entry plan, with precise weekly tasks
- 6-month deep-dive advancement plan, from "can do the work" to "standing firm"
- How to choose learning resources, what's worth paying for, what's free and sufficient

After reading this chapter, you should close the book, open your computer, and start executing.

---

## 3.1 Four Types of Entry Candidates and Their Strategies

Before drawing your roadmap, I need to ask you a question: **Who are you?**

There's no standard answer for entering the AI field. Different starting points determine different optimal paths.

I divide entry candidates into four categories, each with its own advantages and pitfalls. Find your category and your corresponding strategy.

---

### Profile 1: Backend Developers Transitioning to AI Development

**Who You Are**

You have 1-5 years of backend development experience, possibly Java, Go, or Python. You've written APIs, built services, and worked with databases. For you, writing code isn't the problem—the question is "how do I write code on the AI side?"

**Your Advantage**

Your biggest advantage is **engineering capability**. Many people think transitioning to AI means abandoning engineering skills, but it's actually the opposite—the most scarce talent in 2026 is people who can reliably run AI models in production.
