# Chapter 6: RAG - Retrieval Augmented Generation

## What You'll Learn

If Chapter 5 taught you "how to make LLMs listen to you," then this chapter teaches you "how to make LLMs know things they originally didn't."

RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation) is one of the most core technologies in AI application development. When you go for interviews, 80% will ask about RAG; when you build projects, nine out of ten require RAG. The reason is simple: no matter how powerful LLMs are, they have knowledge boundaries—they don't know your company's internal documents, don't understand your unique business processes, and can't use your latest product data. RAG is the solution.

But many students only learned the surface of RAG—knowing vector search, chunking documents casually, tuning a few parameters and thinking that's enough. Interviewers can probe deeper: How do you do reranking? Why would you use semantic chunking instead of recursive chunking in this scenario? What's the difference between Self-RAG and CRAG? How do you handle multimodal documents?

After reading this chapter:

- Deeply understand RAG's essence: not just "retrieval + generation," but a systematic approach to solving knowledge boundary problems
- Master N types of text chunking strategies: recursive, semantic, Parent-Child, and when to use which
- Choose the right Embedding model: Which Chinese Embeddings are best in 2026, when to fine-tune
- Master retrieval strategies: hybrid search, reranking, query rewriting, adaptive retrieval
- Understand advanced RAG architectures: Self-RAG, CRAG, Multi-hop RAG, Agentic RAG, Multimodal RAG
