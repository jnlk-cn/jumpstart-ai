# Chapter 7: AI Agent Core Logic

## What You'll Learn

Congratulations on reaching the final chapter of the Fundamentals section!

If previous chapters taught you "how to use AI," then this chapter teaches you "how to let AI work by itself."

Let me ask you a question: What is the core difference between LLMs and AI Agents?

LLMs are passive—you ask, it answers, question and answer, ceiling is low. AI Agents are proactive—you give it a goal, it figures out how to do it, researches on its own, calls tools, verifies results, until the task is complete.

From "you tell me how to do it" to "you figure it out," this is a qualitative leap.

After reading this chapter, you will take away:

- **Agent essence**: Why Agents are the future of AI applications
- **Agent architecture overview**: The four major components of perception, reasoning, action, and memory
- **Tool calling capabilities**: Let AI operate in the real world (including production-grade code)
- **Planning and reasoning patterns**: ReAct tracking, LangGraph 2026 practice, Plan-and-Execute
- **Multi-Agent collaboration**: Two real usable cases
- **Agent reliability engineering**: Six failure patterns, cost control, output validation
